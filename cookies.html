wow i really like cookies

New York City

Milk and Cookies: so far, my favorite ever - soft, sweet, slightly chewy and very chocolatey. This bakery also makes other delectable bakes like their ridiculously chocolatey brownie
Baked: although this cookie looks good on the outside, I found the texture to be a little too dry and hard for my liking
Levain: world famous and delectable, but not my personal favorite because of the slightly unconventional shape, baking style, and ultimately texture. Also who really goes to midtown all the time
Jacques Torres: a king of kings, beautifully chocolatey, crunchy yet fudgy, and actually enormous. JT will always be my man - don’t miss the mudslide cookie too
Insomnia: a classic and my go-to on a windy winter’s night - reliably fast, warm, and gooey
Max Brenner: potentially one of the worst cookies I’ve ever had in NYC - hard, lacking chocolate, and boring
Rubyzaar Baked: a hidden gem only to be found in Williamsburg on weekends - a gorgeous array of carefully crafted cookies, but the winner in my heart was the Classic Rock for its hearty chocolate chunks and tasteful sprinkle of rock salt
Starbucks: not mind-blowing, but pretty fine for a sugar addict’s morning pick-me-up since it is ALWAYS there. Alternatively, just down with coffee
Schmackary’s: very sweet, yet very beautiful. Fudgy, soft, and rich but a little too sweet for my liking- which says a lot from someone who is actually addicted to sugar
Dominique Ansel: not a cookie, but almost - the cookie shot is filled to the brim with warm milk and is marvellous just to look at. Very fun, but not your everyday cookie - esp. w/ the consistently long lines
Momofuku Milk Bar: in spite world acclaim, don’t come here for the cookie - stick to the icecream
La Colombe: gluten free (if that matters), and almost everywhere around the city. A little crunchier than most - for those who like their fries crispy
Alamo Drafthouse Cinema: really cool, mainly because warm cookies were served to us in a theater as we were watching a movie, but also warm and dense and fragrant
Balthazar Bakery: chewy, chocolatey and pleasant, but not the city’s most memorable cookie
By CHLOE: these vegan cookies are larger than the size of an average human head and are sprinkled all over with chocolate, which is awesome, but just a little too crunchy and thin for my liking
